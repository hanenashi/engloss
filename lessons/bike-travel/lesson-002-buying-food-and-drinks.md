# Lesson 002: Buying Food And Drinks

## Situation

You stop at a small shop during a bike trip. You need food, water, and something quick to eat before riding again.

Your goal: buy simple things politely and ask about price and payment.

## Useful Phrases

- I would like water, please.
- I would like bread, please.
- Do you have sandwiches?
- Do you have bananas?
- How much is this?
- How much is one bottle of water?
- Can I pay by card?
- Can I pay in cash?
- I need something quick.
- I am travelling by bike.

## Tiny Grammar

### I would like...

Use `I would like` for polite buying.

Examples:

- I would like water, please.
- I would like a sandwich, please.
- I would like two bananas, please.

### How much is...?

Use `How much is` to ask about price.

Examples:

- How much is this?
- How much is the sandwich?
- How much is one bottle of water?

### A / An / The

Use `a` for one general thing.

Use `the` when both people know which thing.

Examples:

- I would like a sandwich.
- How much is the sandwich?
- Where is the supermarket?
